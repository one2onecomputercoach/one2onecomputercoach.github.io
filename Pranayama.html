Easy Breathing Techniques

Yogic Breathing is also called "Pranayama", which is one of the Eight Limbs of Yoga.  In Sanskrit, "prana" means breath or life force and "yama" means mastery or to control.  The easy breathing techniques below have a calming effect and are suitable for beginners of any age.  
  
There are many benefits in developing mastery of the breath.  The mind, body, and breath are intimately connected and can influence each other.  Our breathing is influenced by our thoughts, and our thoughts and physiology can be influenced by our breath.  For example, stress or other negative emotions can cause your breathing to become heavy, quick or shallow as your body prepares you for the "fight or flight" response.   When you breathe deeply and slowly, you activate the parasympathetic nervous system, which reverses the stress response in your body.  Learning to breathe consciously and with awareness can be a valuable tool in helping to restore balance in the mind and body.  Other benefits include increased awareness and presence of mind;  increased emotional calm and inner peace; mental clarity and relaxation; and improved circulation, digestion and overall health.

Bee Breath

Bee Breath, called "Bhramari" in Sanskrit, is a breathing technique that helps calm the mind and releases tension within the body. The humming effect brings increased circulation around the upper body, especially the head area. This is a gentle technique that can be done by anyone and is recommended for any time.

Four Part Breath

Four Part Breath, also called Square Breathing, is a simple breath pattern that has a calming effect, can increase lung capacity, reduces stagnation in the lungs, and can be done by any living being. While normal breathing typically only focuses on the inhale and exhale, the Four Part Breath focuses on breaking down the breath into these four parts:
1. Inhalation "Puraka"
2. Internal Retention "Antara Kumbhaka"
3. Exhalation "Recaka"
4. External Retention "Bahya Kumbhaka"
