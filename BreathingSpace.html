Breathing Space


Yogic Breathing, also called "Pranayama", is one of the Eight Limbs of Yoga. In Sanskrit, "prana" means breath or life force and "yama" means mastery or to control. The easy breathing techniques below have a calming effect and are suitable for beginners of all ages.  

There are many benefits in developing mastery of the breath. The mind, body, and breath are intimately connected and can influence each other. Our breathing is influenced by our thoughts, and our thoughts and physiology can be influenced by our breathing. For example, stress or other negative emotions can cause your breathing to become heavy, quick or shallow as your body prepares you for the "fight or flight" response.  By mindfully breathing deeply and slowly you activate the parasympathetic nervous system, reversing the stress response in the body. Learning to breathe consciously and with awareness can be a valuable tool in helping to restore balance in the mind and body. Other benefits can include increased awareness and presence of mind; increased emotional calm and inner peace; mental clarity and relaxation; and improved circulation, digestion and overall health.


"Breathing in, I calm body and mind. Breathing out, I smile. Dwelling in the present moment I know this is the only moment.” – Thich Nhat Hanh

Bee Breath

Bee Breath, called "Bhramari" in Sanskrit, is a breathing technique that helps calm the mind and release tension from within the body. The humming effect increases circulation in the upper body, especially around the head area. This easy and gentle technique can be done by anyone and is recommended for any time.

Four Part Breath

Four Part Breath, also called Square Breathing, is a simple breath pattern that has a calming effect, can increase lung capacity, reduces stagnation in the lungs, and can be done by any living being. While normal breathing typically only focuses on the inhale and exhale, the Four Part Breath focuses on breaking down the breath into these four parts:  Inhalation "Puraka"; Internal Retention "Antara Kumbhaka"; Exhalation "Recaka"; and External Retention "Bahya Kumbhaka".
